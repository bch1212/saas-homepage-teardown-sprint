# Sample Teardown Report — AcmeOps

> Demo sample for the SaaS Homepage Teardown Sprint. This is not a report about a real buyer.

## Snapshot

- **Homepage:** https://example.com
- **Target customer:** ops leaders at 50–500 person B2B SaaS companies
- **Primary conversion goal:** book a demo
- **Current hero:** “AI-powered workflows for modern teams”
- **Current CTA:** “Get started”

## Executive read

The page sounds modern, but the first screen does not yet answer three buyer questions fast enough:

1. What workflow pain does this remove?
2. Which team owns the pain?
3. Why should I book a demo instead of assuming this is another generic AI automation tool?

## Above-the-fold friction

### 1. The hero headline is category-generic

“AI-powered workflows for modern teams” could describe dozens of products. The buyer has to scroll or infer the actual use case.

**Fix direction:** name the painful workflow and the buyer.

Potential rewrites:

- “Cut SaaS ops handoff delays with AI-assisted workflow routing.”
- “Turn messy customer handoffs into tracked ops workflows.”
- “Give RevOps and Customer Success one place to route every post-sale task.”

### 2. The CTA does not match a B2B buying motion

“Get started” implies self-serve signup, but if the real goal is demo booking, the CTA should set expectations.

**Fix direction:** use a specific action.

- Primary: “Book a 20-minute workflow audit”
- Secondary: “See sample ops workflow”

### 3. Trust proof appears too late

If there are customer logos, integration names, security proof, or quantified time savings, at least one proof point should be visible before the first scroll.

**Fix direction:** add one compact proof row under the CTA:

> Used by CS and RevOps teams to route handoffs across Slack, HubSpot, Salesforce, and Jira.

## Recommended page structure

1. Hero: painful workflow + buyer + specific CTA.
2. Proof strip: integrations, logos, or quantified operating metric.
3. Before/after workflow visual.
4. Three high-friction use cases.
5. Demo CTA repeated with context.
6. Objection handling: setup time, security, integrations.

## Prioritized 10-point fix list

| Priority | Fix | Impact | Effort |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Rewrite H1 around painful workflow, not broad AI category | High | Low |
| 2 | Replace “Get started” with demo-specific CTA | High | Low |
| 3 | Add one proof line under CTA | High | Low |
| 4 | Show a simple before/after workflow diagram | High | Medium |
| 5 | Name the exact buyer persona in the subhead | Medium | Low |
| 6 | Move integrations above first major scroll | Medium | Low |
| 7 | Add one outcome metric or customer quote | Medium | Medium |
| 8 | Add “how it works” in 3 steps | Medium | Medium |
| 9 | Use secondary CTA for sample workflow | Medium | Low |
| 10 | Add FAQ for implementation/security objections | Medium | Medium |

## Suggested first implementation pass

Change only the hero for a 24-hour test:

**H1:** Turn messy customer handoffs into tracked ops workflows  
**Subhead:** AcmeOps helps RevOps and Customer Success teams route post-sale tasks across Slack, HubSpot, Salesforce, and Jira — without another spreadsheet.  
**Primary CTA:** Book a 20-minute workflow audit  
**Secondary CTA:** See a sample handoff workflow

This teardown identifies likely clarity and conversion friction based on page review. It does not guarantee conversion lift or revenue outcomes.
