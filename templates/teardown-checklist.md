# Teardown Checklist

Score each section 1–5. The report should focus on the highest-impact low-effort fixes.

## 1. First-impression clarity

- Can a visitor describe the product category in 5 seconds?
- Is the buyer persona obvious?
- Is the painful before-state named?
- Is the desired after-state concrete?

## 2. Hero message

- H1 avoids generic AI/productivity language.
- Subhead explains who, what, and outcome.
- CTA matches the buying motion.
- Secondary CTA handles lower-intent visitors.

## 3. ICP and positioning

- Page chooses a narrow initial audience.
- Differentiator is visible before competitor comparison is required.
- Use cases support one story instead of many unrelated verticals.

## 4. Trust and proof

- Logos, quotes, metrics, integrations, security, or founder credibility are visible early.
- Claims are specific and supportable.
- No unsupported guaranteed revenue/conversion claims.

## 5. CTA path

- Primary CTA is repeated after key sections.
- Demo/signup expectation is clear.
- Form friction is proportional to buyer intent.
- Pricing/demo/contact path is findable.

## 6. Objection handling

- Implementation time.
- Integrations.
- Security/data handling if relevant.
- Team adoption.
- Why now / why switch.

## 7. Copy mechanics

- One dominant H1.
- Concrete nouns and verbs.
- Minimal jargon.
- Benefits tied to buyer-owned metrics.

## 8. Final report output

Include:

1. Executive read.
2. Three biggest friction points.
3. Hero rewrite options.
4. CTA/proof recommendations.
5. Prioritized 10-point fix list.
6. One 24-hour implementation pass.
7. Non-guarantee note.
